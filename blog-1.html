Quarry Mining Should Increase Productivity

  • Author
  • Nov 15, 2017
  • 1
  • 3

Quary Mining is a mining method that included as the open pit system, but has some difference with the open pit mining method. Quarry mining is characterized by the type of excavated commodities ie non-metallic rock material which has lower selling value relatively than metal ore such as gold, silver, copper, and others. Types of quarry commodities produced by quarries include excavated materials for construction (sand, gravel, etc), granite, gypsum, limestone, and others. In addition, other characterization of quarry mining are small-medium acale, relatively shallow deposit location, relatively strong rock mass characteristics, steep slopes and relatively high material excavation cost (including blasting) (Darling, 2011). Generally, quarry mining products are sold off-site and due to low selling value and make products arenâ€™t economical to transport in long distance. This cause more of quarry mining emerging on a small-medium scale to meet local market demand. In quarries with certain commodities such as limestone quarry integrated with the plant or with a super massive reserve, management can increase mining scale due to an improved economy.

In the limestone quarry mine integrated with the cement factory, quicklime or other factories, there are challenges in mining operations that are in terms of productivity of mining operations. According to Hartman (1987), one of the challenges of the quarry mining method is the relatively lower productivity compared to other mining methods. This low productivity can be analyzed as a result of strong rock mass characteristics, so to perform excavation activities of materials with larger equipment (excavation capacity) or by more powerful / modern blasting techniques, more expensive investment / cost is required and cause mining operations as a whole become less economical (especially in small-medium quarry quarries). In fact, the rate of quarry mining production is essential to ensure consistency and continuity of mining products as factories demand.
